About Electroverse

Electroverse.Comm-TSEC, a Technical Committee of the EXTC Department at Thadomal Shahani Engineering College, Mumbai, Maharashtra, cordially invites you to TECH-A-THON 2023, a 48-hour Hardware Hackathon. This dynamic event promises a deep dive into the Vortex of Technovation, .. challenging participants to showcase their potential in providing practical solutions to real-world issues with a Technical Edge. The hackathon emphasizes a hardware-focused approach, encouraging participants to delve into tangible innovation. Attendees can anticipate networking opportunities, skill enhancement, and the chance to win prizes while connecting with industry professionals and mentors. Whether you're a coding expert or a hardware aficionado, TECH-A-THON welcomes all technology enthusiasts to join this transformative experience. Register now for a journey where ideas come to life, and innovation takes center stage.

About College

Thadomal Shahani Engineering College (TSEC) in Mumbai, India, established in 1983, is the first private engineering institute affiliated with the University of Mumbai. Founded by the Hyderabad (Sind) National Collegiate Board, it is named after philanthropist Thadomal Shahani's father. .. Approved by the Directorate of Technical Education of Maharashtra in 1983, TSEC is among India's oldest private engineering colleges, pioneering undergraduate studies in computer engineering, information technology, and biomedical engineering. Notably, the Department of Biomedical Engineering, established in 1983, is one of India's oldest. TSEC expanded its offerings over the years, introducing programs such as Artificial Intelligence and Data Science in 2020.

About Department

The Department of Electronics and Telecommunication at TSEC was established in 2002, aiming to excel in telecommunications and adapt to rapid advancements in communication-related fields. Formerly known as Electronics Engineering (1983-2001), ... it has maintained a student intake of 60 since 2002. The department has received continuous accreditation from the National Board of Accreditation since 2008 and recently from NAAC. Initiatives include a PhD program since 2014-15, focusing on holistic learning and offering diverse electives like Neural Networks, VLSI, Artificial Intelligence, and more. Boasting state-of-the-art labs, the department emphasizes excellence in telecommunication

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can participate in this Hackathon?

Participants can be a student of any year of the B.E.(Bachelor of Engineering)/ B.Tech Degree college or any Technical Diploma College.

Wrong details are submitted in the registration form, how can they be corrected?

Contact the Student Coordinators (Contact details are mentioned on the next page) or mail to electroversecommtsec@gmail.com

Will Food and wireless internet access be provided?

Yes, there will be. However, it is always better to be prepared: If you have a backup option, please feel free to bring it along and Food, Lunch, Snacks, and Dinner will be provided throughout the event.

What should teams carry to the Hackathon Venue?

Students are requested to bring their own devices and other necessary equipment required for the event. The components required to build the solution are to be informed by the qualified Teams after Round 1, which will be arranged for them by the Thadomal Shahani Engineering College.

What is the benefit of participating?

Self-assess your abilities while coordinating with your team, Get tips from the Experts, enhance social skills, seize the opportunity to win prize money of up to Rs 25,000/- and have fun along with an effective and memorable learning experience.

Is it mandatory to form teams with all members from the same college?

No, you can form your team with all members who qualify the eligibility criteria.
